Unit 1: Introduction to Research Methods. The Scientific Investigation & Ethics in Computing

Unit 1 educated me on the purpose of research; research is twofold for any student, regardless of the level they are pursuing as it can help improve critical thinking, understanding of a particular topic and most importantly, make evidence-based decisions. Research is classified into three purposes; exploration, description and explanation (Singh, 2019).

The use of the scientific method for research is classified in two categories; deductive and inductive with the major difference being the order of the process. Deductive reasoning starts with the theory, developing a hypothesis, collecting and analyzing data to test the hypothesis, whereas inductive reasoning starts with collecting and analyzing data, and stating the hypothesis (Decarlo, 2018).

Unit 3: Methodology & Research Methods

Research methods are the methods in which data is collected and analyzed during the data gathering phase of a project, most times the method of research is directly related to the research question posed by the researcher (Henderson, 2015). The methods are classified into qualitative, quantitative and a combination of both. The qualitative method consists of collecting and analyzing non numerical data in an attempt to understand individuals‚Äô experiences and opinions, it is particularly useful to answer questions of ‚Äúhow‚Äù or ‚Äúwhy,‚Äù and is particularly useful for real world problems (Tenny et al, 2022).

The quantitative method however, deals with statistics acquired through surveys and questionnaires in an attempt to find patterns, make predictions and finding the central tendency of data (Rana et al, 2021). Research Design however deals with the way in which the research is conducted and how the findings are acquired, it includes the ‚Äúhow‚Äù in order to undertake research for the chosen topic (Khanday & Khanam, 2023). There are two types of research design, exploratory research and conclusive research. Exploratory research deals with a selected topic narrowed down sufficiently however there is not a clear definition of what the issue is, however the outcome of this type of research is ultimately to further the understanding or provide additional information. Conclusive research aims to give a definitive answer to a more focused area (BRM, n.d.).

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups & Observations

Unit 4 expanded on the qualitative and quantitative data collection methods which include observation, focus groups and case studies. Each of these had its strengths and offered unique viewpoints which I will expand on.

The observation method focuses on describing the behavior of an object until there has been some discernable insight achieved (Kumar, 2023). The observation method is the most common method used, as it is a subconscious act that occurs, even outside of professional and academic settings.

The focus group method is primarily a qualitative research method where a group of people from similar demographics are collectively interviewed in an attempt to have open dialogue and allow for lines of communication to occur for collaboration between all participants (Jarvis & Barberena, 2008).

The case study is a very specific study of a particular topic with the aim of generalizing across a larger topic. It is used for defining scenarios, rather than analyzing them in great detail (Gerring, 2004).

Unit 5: Interviews, Survey Methods & Questionnaire Design

The interview method is a qualitative method of research that involves asking open ended questions to an individual or group of individuals in an attempt to garner insight into the particular topic (Trainor, 2012). The use of open-ended questions allows for a dialogue between the interviewer and interviewee to allow for a more casual interaction to occur (Qu & Dumay, 2011). Surveys are an alternative method of gathering information from large groups of individuals which can be summarized and used to create visualizations for an easy way of expressing large amounts of information (Anheier, & Scherer, 2015).

The questionnaire design is important to the type of research being conducted; specifically the type of questions whether it be opened or closed in order to remove any ambiguity to the respondent which minimizes bias (Malhotra, 2006).

Unit 6: Quantitative Methods - Descriptive & Inferential Statistics

Unit 6 deals with quantitative methods of research which uses numerical variables which have been collected in order to analyze and gain results to make decisions (Apuke, 2017). The importance of quantitative research to the individual and topic undertaken is the fact that the data obtained is objective and reliable, which is used for making informed decisions. The reliability of this data is due to standardized procedures and statistical techniques which can be replicated (Ghanad, 2023). Through the use of statistics and visualization techniques, data can be summarized and valuable information can be garnered.

There are a variety of data collection methods which can be used, however there are six major types which include; questionnaires, interviews, focus groups tests, observations and secondary data (which is data utilized from another source. The use of multiple forms of data collection is ideal as it allows for a mixed method to ensure that there are complementary strengths, and no overlapping weaknesses (Tashakkori & Teddlie, 2013).
